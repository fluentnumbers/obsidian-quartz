# [[How to kindly request the best interview feedback]]
<sub>scroll ↓ to [[#Resources]]</sub>

## Note
The [[GDPR]] gives individuals the "right to be informed". You practice it every day by hastily getting rid of yet another "consent for cookies" banner.
▶️ But there are also other rights - for access, rectification, deletion - which can be pro-actively exercised. For instance, some people request to download\delete ALL their account info from social networks, travel websites, etc.

▶️ But here is another tip:
A while ago I applied for a job and have had four interviews (+ HR call) after which followed an automated rejection email. And further no reply to my kind request for feedback.
I contacted the company's data protection office and requested all information they have about me regarding this encounter. After a month I received a zip file with the most comprehensive and useful feedback ever. Split by interviewer\stage, redacted, but with obvious references to the competences and topics discussed. Really top-level diligence on their side despite smth was broken in the hiring process.

▶️ This will work with big and small companies as far as you are a resident of the EU. The only downside is that companies with no formal hiring process can send back only your resume.pdf back.

P.S. Try a browser extension "I still don't care about cookies", changes your life for the best.
